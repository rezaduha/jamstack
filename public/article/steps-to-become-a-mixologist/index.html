Steps to Become a Mixologist

By James Anthony Knight Published on May 27, 2022

Enrolling in a hotel or hospitality management course is a great career path and opens up a plethora of avenues for you but what it lacks is specialization; something which is becoming exceedingly important in today’s day and age. This is why there exist courses which train you in a specific skill and is a sure shot way of landing a job in that field. One such popular and in demand skill is that of a mixologist also referred to as a bartender or a sommelier.

Are you interested in mixing spirits and making a career out of it? Read on to find out more about how to become a mixologist in a few easy steps.

What exactly does a mixologist do?

A mixologist is someone who sits behind the bar counter and mixes your favorite spirits and flavours to create just the right drink for every customer. It is an art which requires years of practice to master it to the finest details and involves a lot of experimenting to devise special in-house cocktails. The most common sectors which employ mixologists are Restaurant cum bar and pubs, Luxury hotels and resorts, Cruise lines, Private parties such as corporate events and weddings.

What are the steps to become a successful mixologist?

1. Get Certified

Although formal education is not compulsory to make a career as a mixologist, it will definitely be a plus point in your CV. Your experience and certification from a reputable institute will guarantee you a decent job. Enrolling in a specialised mixology course leads to networking and helps build connections for your future.

2. Experience Matters

When it comes to such a practical art as mixology, your years of experience matter a lot to your prospective employers. You may start out as a bartender of your local bar or even as a server and observe and learn from your peers. This will help you gain exposure and give you a practical hands-on experience of the real world scenario.

3. Practice Using Professional Tools

Invest in good professional tools required for mixology such as long-lasting, easy to clean, rust proof glasses and set up a mini bar at home where you can practice. There are complicated drinks where even the slightest miscalculations can result in a blunder. Mixology is an art and much like any artform you must keep practising it to remain proficient in it.

4. Other Essential Skills

Besides these technical skills, a mixologist must also possess a variety of soft skills that will help in your job. These may include:

  • Multitasking: A mixologist is required to prepare drinks for many clients at the same time so excellent multitasking skills is a must have.
  • Creativity: Sometimes clients may demand a special drink which is not listed in the menu and in such a case your creativity comes into play and the reputation of the establishment is on your shoulders.
  • Quality customer service: In the hospitality industry nothing is more important than guest experience. A mixologist must be polite and engage with customers about the choice of their spirits and retain their preference to create repeat business for the future.
  • Mental and physical strength: A mixologist must remember the various combinations involved in hundreds of cocktails as well as be on his feet for hours at a stretch thus testing both his physical as well as mental toughness.
